# Heading — first line of the story

Open with a scene or a hook — what was the problem, who walked in with it, what was at stake.

## The client

Who they are, how long they have been around, who they serve. Keep it human. If numbers matter (members, years, villages), use them — but only the numbers the client has approved for publication.

## The build

What we built and how. Concrete tech names: React, GKE Autopilot, Razorpay, DigiLocker, PostgreSQL. Timeline. Team size. Any constraint that shaped the design.

## The handover

What the client received at the end of Phase N — code, docs, training, research. A one-line outcome metric if one is available ("portal live at X in 14 days").

## What this proves

The broader point this story illustrates about Aksha's thesis — AI-augmented delivery, cost compression, quality-at-small-scale, whatever applies.

## What comes next

Phase 2 scope, or the next client the work opens up.
